Research: Machine Learning, Optimal Transport

My research aims to improve scalability, efficiency, and robustness for learning with large-scale, complex data and models from optimal transport (OT) theory perspective, focusing on two following objectives:

  • (i) Deepening OT theory: break the curse of dimensionality for OT with 1D-structure; and going beyond Lp structure for variational Wasserstein problems.

  • (ii) Leveraging OT theoretical insights for data science: designing innovative efficient methodologies; and improving efficiency, scalability, and robustness for applications with large-scale, complex data.

My research is structured across three levels:

  • Theory: OT theory for measures with graph structure, tree systems, functional geometric structure.

  • Methodology: scalability, robustness, and efficient of OT for learning with measures in machine learning and deep learning, e.g., generative models, diffusion models, data valuation for LLM pretraining.

  • Application: for domains with large-scale, complex structure data, including non-Euclidean, high-dimension, dynamic, nonlinearity, corruption.

Also, I actively expand the research spectrum to interesting and impactful (related) research problems.
